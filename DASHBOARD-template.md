## 🏆 GRAND PRIX — Best of the Best

### Lloyds Bank — "Bank on Lloyds"
**Why it's great:** A masterful brand repositioning that transformed a traditional bank into an enabler of personal ambition. The insight that 75% of UK adults want progress in 2026 made this deeply relatable. 21.3M app users gave the promise credibility.
**Agency:** In-house + Partner Agencies | **Category:** Brand Transformation

### Coca-Cola / Pepsi — "The Choice"
**Why it's great:** Pepsi turned the iconic Coca-Cola polar bear into their unwitting mascot with witty existential humor. The bear's journey of self-discovery after choosing Pepsi is cheeky Super Bowl gold.
**Agency:** BBDO | **Category:** TV / Super Bowl 60

### Jacquemus — "Liline, Brand Ambassador"
**Why it's great:** Appointed the founder's 88-year-old grandmother as brand ambassador — the most authentic move luxury fashion has seen in years. The "contract" went viral because it subverted every expectation.
**Category:** Social / OOH | **Why:** Authentic storytelling beats celebrity endorsements

## 🎬 TV / CINEMA — Best Commercials

### Liquid I.V. — "Take a Look" 
**Why:** Phil Collins' "Against All Odds" sung by a chorus of toilets beckoning you to judge your pee color. Memorable, educative, and absurd in the best way.
**Agency:** Anomaly | Watch: [iSpot.tv](https://www.ispot.tv)

### Squarespace — "Unavailable"
**Why:** Emma Stone + director Yorgos Lanthimos = dark absurdist gold. 30 seconds of the weirdly wonderful sensibility that defined their film collaborations, bottled for Super Bowl.
**Agency:** In-house

### TurboTax — "The Expert" 
**Why:** Adrien Brody leans hard into "pretentious Oscar winner" character, begging to "tap into the pain of tax season" while the director just wants him to dial it down. Self-aware celebrity parody at its best.
**Agency:** R/GA

### Anthropic — "A Time and a Place"
**Why:** Sam Altman's biggest rival runs ads mocking Sam Altman. Darkly funny AI humor that sparked genuine debate about whether the company will keep its "ad-free" promise.
**Agency:** Mother

## 📰 PRINT / OUT-OF-HOME

### Stella Artois — Snow Billboard
**Why:** Real snow fills an oversized chalice, creating the illusion of frothy beer foam spilling over the billboard. Contextual genius — activated during record snowfall in Toronto.
**Agency:** In-house

### IKEA — Flat-Pack City Campaign  
**Why:** Wrapped Copenhagen in iconic flat-pack graphics to make a big point: bulky items now in stock at city-centre stores. Simple visual that tells a practical story.
**Agency:** Unknown

### Audi — Weather-Triggered DOOH
**Why:** Thermometer visuals activated by real-time temperature drops. Positions AWD technology as synonymous with winter readiness — timing is everything.
**Agency:** BBH + PHD

### Heinz — "Ketchup-Friendly Fry Box"
**Why:** After 75 years, finally redesigned the fry box with a dedicated ketchup dipping section. Product innovation that became the entire campaign — no additional storytelling needed.
**Agency:** Rethink

## 💻 ONLINE / DIGITAL

### Unilever Vaseline — Social Listening Campaign
**Why:** Monitored TikTok trends and community conversations in real-time, responding to user-generated hacks with expert validation. Audiences became the creative brief.
**Channel:** TikTok, Creator Economy

### OpenAI ChatGPT — "Everyday AI"
**Why:** Warm, relatable everyday moments instead of sci-fi future angles. Repositioned AI from intimidating to accessible — critical before launching ads inside ChatGPT itself.
**Category:** Brand Identity / Tech

### Bumble — Real Connections
**Why:** Pete Davidson + Elsie Hewitt in a social-first storytelling series about genuine human connection. Exactly what younger audiences crave in an AI-saturated content environment.
**Agency:** In-house | **Category:** Dating App / Social
